Classic of Mountains and Seas consists of 18 volumes and about 31,000 words, and consists of two main parts, the Mountain Sutra and the Sea Sutra, and is subdivided into four main parts, namely. The Classic of Mountains and Seas, written between the Warring States period and the early Han dynasty, is known as one of the three great books of the ancient world, together with the I Ching and the Yellow Emperor's Classic of Internal Medicine.

It contains many contents about ancient geography, history, mythology, astronomy, animals, plants, medicine, religion, as well as anthropology, ethnology, oceanography and the history of science and technology, and is an encyclopaedia of the life of ancient society. The Classic of Mountains and Seas shows the ancient culture, records the living conditions and the activities of people's minds during the Great Wilderness period, outlines the state of civilization and culture in the ancient times, and provides a lot of useful information for future generations.
